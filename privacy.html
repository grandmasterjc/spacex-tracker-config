1. Overview

This Privacy Policy describes how Joachim Gresslien / bitfactory ("we", "us", or "our") handles information when you use the SpaceX Tracker iOS application ("the app").

We are based in Norway and operate within the European Economic Area (EEA). The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) applies to our processing. If you are located in California, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) also applies — your rights under CCPA are described in Section 10.

We collect only what is necessary to operate the app and deliver push notifications. We do not sell personal data. We do not use advertising identifiers (IDFA).

3. Firebase Analytics

The app uses Firebase Analytics (Google LLC) to understand how the app is used and to improve stability and features. Firebase Analytics collects data automatically when the app runs.

Events collected

  • first_open — app launched for the first time after install
  • app_update — app updated to a new version
  • app_remove — app uninstalled (reported before removal)
  • session_start — a new usage session begins
  • screen_view — user navigates to a screen
  • notification_receive — a push notification is delivered
  • notification_foreground — a push notification is received while the app is open
  • notification_open — user taps a push notification

User properties collected

  • Country — derived from IP address at collection time; the raw IP address is not stored by Firebase
  • Language — device locale setting
  • App version — installed version string
  • Device model — e.g., iPhone 15 Pro

Device identifier

Firebase Analytics uses the IDFV (Identifier for Vendors), a device-level identifier assigned by iOS. The IDFV is shared across apps from the same developer (bitfactory) and resets when all apps from that developer are uninstalled. The app does not use the IDFA (Identifier for Advertisers) and advertising is disabled in the Firebase configuration (IS_ADS_ENABLED = false).

Legal basis (GDPR)

We process analytics data under Legitimate Interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) to understand aggregate app usage and improve the user experience. We limit collection to the events listed above and have disabled advertising features.

Opting out

You can opt out of Firebase Analytics data collection at any time by going to iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements and turning off Share iPhone Analytics. Alternatively, contact us at the email in Section 14 and we will provide guidance.

4. Push Notifications (Firebase Cloud Messaging)

If you allow notifications, the app registers with Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) to receive push notifications about launch updates, schedule changes, and mission outcomes.

  • FCM registration token — a device-level token generated by Firebase and stored on Firebase servers. It is used solely to route notifications to your device. It does not contain your name, email, or any personally identifying information.
  • Topic subscription — the app subscribes to the topic all_users. All subscribers receive the same notifications. There are no user-specific or identifying topics.

Notifications are delivered through Apple Push Notification service (APNs). Apple acts as a data processor for this delivery. See Section 8 for Apple's privacy policy link.

The FCM token is automatically rotated by Firebase periodically. When you delete the app, the token is effectively deregistered and can no longer receive notifications.

Legal basis (GDPR)

Push notification registration is based on your consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR) given when iOS prompts you to allow notifications. You can withdraw consent at any time in iOS Settings → Notifications → SpaceX Tracker.

5. In-App Purchases

Subscriptions and one-time purchases are processed entirely by Apple via StoreKit 2. We do not collect, see, or store your payment card details, billing address, or Apple ID.

The app receives a StoreKit 2 transaction receipt to verify subscription status. This status (active / inactive) is cached locally on your device in UserDefaults. It is not transmitted to our servers.

All purchase-related inquiries (refunds, billing issues) must be directed to Apple.

6. Local Storage

The app caches the following data locally on your device to provide a fast, offline-capable experience:

  • News articles fetched from the content repository
  • Merch listings
  • Launch schedules and mission details from the Launch Library 2 API
  • User preferences (notification settings, display preferences)
  • Subscription status (see Section 5)

None of this locally stored data is transmitted to us or any third party. It is deleted when you delete the app.

10. Your Rights (GDPR & CCPA)

Rights under GDPR (EEA users)

Under the General Data Protection Regulation you have the right to:

  • Access — request a copy of personal data we hold about you
  • Rectification — request correction of inaccurate data
  • Erasure — request deletion of your personal data
  • Restriction — request that we limit processing of your data
  • Portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Object — object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent (push notifications), withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority. In Norway, this is the Datatilsynet.

Rights under CCPA (California users)

California residents have the right to:

  • Know what personal information is collected and how it is used
  • Request deletion of personal information
  • Opt out of the sale of personal information — we do not sell personal information
  • Non-discrimination for exercising privacy rights

How to exercise your rights

For data held by us: contact us at the email address in Section 14. We will respond within 30 days.

For data held by Google (Firebase Analytics/FCM): use Google's Data & Privacy tools or contact Google directly.

Deleting your data

The most effective way to remove locally stored data and your FCM token is to delete the app from your device. Analytics data held by Google is anonymised in aggregate after the 14-month retention period.

Opting out of analytics

Go to iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements and turn off Share iPhone Analytics. This prevents Firebase Analytics from collecting events on your device.
